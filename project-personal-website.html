Personal website + automated publishing system

June 2026 · AI-native, Web, Automation

A one-stop professional home on the web — designed, built, and deployed through an AI-native workflow, with publishing and monitoring automated from day one.

Context

I wanted a single, fast, minimalist destination that introduces me professionally and links my social profiles — and that could grow over time without manual upkeep. The goal was less “a website” and more “a system”: something that keeps itself current.

What I did

  • Benchmarked best-in-class minimalist personal sites and locked a monochrome, content-first design with dark mode and strong performance/accessibility baselines.
  • Shipped a static site on free, durable hosting with automatic HTTPS — chosen so publishing is a simple, version-controlled push.
  • Built an auto-publish flow: when a project wraps, a concise summary can be drafted to an approved template and published here with one approval.
  • Set up daily uptime monitoring with diagnosed causes and an independent always-on external monitor.

Outcome

  • A live, mobile-first site that loads fast and links cleanly to LinkedIn and X.
  • A repeatable pipeline so new projects and articles publish themselves with minimal effort.
